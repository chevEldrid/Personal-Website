Weekly Update

January 31, 2018

After spending the last month or so in Upstate New York, I think I'm ready to travel halfway around the world. In another week or so my family and I will begin our trip to Australia and New Zealand where I'll be spending the Spring semester! So keep an eye on the travel blog for updates on the trip. I'll use this site to post pictures and various small things but the bulk of content will be located on the other site.

AWS

Three weeks of intense studying and note taking later, the test for "AWS Certified Solutions Architect - Associate" is behind me. I passed! (This allows me to claim entry-level knowledge in designing IT frameworks and infrastructure on the AWS platform for two years, and then I'll need to renew.) I don't want to relax just yet though, while this certification is a very important one, it doesn't quite line up with my career aspirations...which is why I hope in the coming weeks to begin the AWS Developer - Associate exam training. While I am familiar with the core build of AWS, using it for designing and implementing code is sort of another beast. From what I'm told, the overlap between the two exams is pretty substantial so fingers crossed the time commitment will be diminished. Thanks again to the awesome folks at A Cloud Guru for the course!

Snowfall

So, I will admit there has been a little bit of neglect on my end these past couple weeks but not as much as this site reflects! While testing out some of the cooler parts of input-driven Javascript is easy enough with a psuedo-scripted adventure sequence, it's harder to translate that into a fun adventure. I may have the technical understanding of how to write scenarios with objects and characters and story progression...but it all requires knowledge of the specific items, people and places to write about.

I'm hard at work on the latter, trying to make sure this adventure is as fun to play as it is to write. More on the technical side though, I'm really excited to go more in-depth with the decision engine that parses user interactions so I think that'll be a talking point for next week.

That's about all I got for this week! Tune in next time for some seriously cool Javascript!
