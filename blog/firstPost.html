Going Live

October 23, 2017

So I've toyed with the idea of making a website for a long time now...seems like the thing to do nowadays. With that in mind, a couple weeks ago I finally took up the challange. (This may or may not have had anything to do with how out of depth I felt while aiding in the design of my Fraternity's website...) We're still working out the kinks. In fact, this little project started off really as a way to test what features we could implement in the future with ease and then, well, here we are.

The Beginning

As far as I was concerned, the web was as simple as a single html document, a style sheet, and maybe a little js if you were feeling fancy. I came into this soley with the tutorials of sites like Solo Learn and CodeCademy under my belt. Needless to say the beginning was a little bit of a bigger learning curve than I expected, but soon I started getting the hang of it. Admittedly I cheated a little, taking inspiration from various other amazing coding blogs and using a bootstrap template, but I'm really proud of this little guy.

The Plan

So I think the idea is to write a little about my current coding projects that could range anywhere from website design to app development and maybe some thoughtful insight into best practices. Perhaps this can serve as a way to link other beginning programmers to material on the web that could really make their lives easier. With that, let's get to it!
