Going Live

October 23, 2017

I've toyed with the idea of making a website for a long time now, seems like the thing to do nowadays, but kept getting deterred by my lack of web experience. I knew I wanted to build the site from scratch, but was terrified about the general lack of polish or detail such an endeavor might result in. It took a rather hefty nudge being drafted to help build my Fraternity's website that jumpstarted my willingness to give it a go. During this process I was introduced to things like Bootstrap, or the idea of "if you're not really sure how to do something, start with a template from someone who does." In fact, this website mostly started off as a way to test what features we could implement into the Northeastern FIJI website and then, well, here we are.

The Beginning

My entirety of web experience before this point entailed soley of the tutorials from sites like Solo Learn and CodeCademy. The beginning was a little bit of a bigger learning curve than I expected, but soon I started getting the hang of it. Admittedly I cheated a little, taking inspiration from various other amazing coding blogs, but I'm really proud of this site.

The Plan

So I think the idea is to write a little about my current coding projects that could range anywhere from website design to app development and maybe some thoughtful insight into best practices I've come across and siting other examples here as well. Perhaps this can even serve as a way to link other beginning programmers to material on the web that could really make their lives easier. With that, let's get to it!
