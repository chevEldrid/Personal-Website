All Good Things in Moderation

October 25, 2017

Recently, I read this great article on something refered to as Learner's Syndrome: a necessity to always be learning...and I completely identify with that. While maybe not to the level of trying to comprehend the fast jungle that is the web, I do find myself bouncing around a lot as soon as I hear of a cool new language or project idea. Even this website, dreamed up last week and already I've poured hours and hours into its construction. I've learned about Bootstrap, Boilerplate, and some small amounts of generic javascript. Even Node.js is looking pretty interesting...But I know I need to slow down.

That being said...

I'm really happy with the progress these last couple days. While a lot of the basic content has remained the same (layout, page content, pictures), I've been able to add custom fonts from Google using Google Fonts, further styling the individual sections of the blog through CSS, and fixing the images to be the Goldilocks size. The original pictures I took on my phone camera were around maybe a megabyte and a half. This is apparently a really big size for pictures and watching them load on mobile was a struggle. In my first attempt, I over-compensated and shrunk the images down to miniscule size using Gimp. This 600x400 proved to be far too pixely in the headers so I went instead for half the original dimensions. I've also decided to include the "title" attribute on all of the site's pictures, giving a little detail to location after stripping the more personal metadata from the picture.

Biggest new Upcoming feature

I think a big part of me wants to push the blog portion of the site at the cost of my self-promotion. This site needs to be able to explain me too so I'm going to spend some time making the "About" page pretty freaking cool, and that starts with a picture-based resume. Instead of having a download link, I hope to move to a grid of photos where clicking on each individual icon will update specific text about what that image stands for. This would make my credentials a more dynamic process and more enjoyable than a slip of paper. All that, and easy buttons to switch between posts in order.
